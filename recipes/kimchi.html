Mak-Kimchi

Ingredients (for one cabbage's worth):

  • 1 nappa cabbage (~xkg)
  • 1/2 cup salt
  • 250ml water, for the porridge
  • 3tbsp rice flour, for the porridge
  • 1tbsp sugar, for the porridge
  • 1 thumb ginger
  • 1/2 head of garlic
  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 cup red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 150ml fish sauce
  • 3 green onions
  • 1 small leek
  • 2 small carrots
  • Daikon radish

Method:

  1. Cut the cabbage lengthwise into quarters, then perpendicularly into ~3cm wide pieces.
  2. Soak the cabbage in a large bowl with cold water and the salt for 1h30mins, turning the cabbage over every 30mins.
  3. Rinse the cabbage under cold water 3 times to clean it.
  4. Drain and set aside.
  5. Make the rice porridge by boling the water, rice flour and sugar in a pot, stirring until it becomes translucent.
  6. Let cool before using
  7. In a food processor, add the garlic, ginger and onion, and finely mince.
  8. Then add the porridge, red pepper flakes, and fish sauce until a paste has been made.
  9. Put the paste in a small bowl.
  10. Add the julienned leek, carrots, and radish to the paste, as well as the thinly sliced green onions. Mix well.
  11. Mix the paste thoroughly into the cabbage, using your hands to ensure it's all well coated.
  12. Set aside in an airtight container, and let it ferment in the fridge for at least a week before you start using it.
